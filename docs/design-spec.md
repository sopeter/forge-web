# Forge Marketing Site — Design Spec
**Date:** 2026-04-12
**Status:** Approved for implementation

---

## Overview

A single-page marketing website for the Forge iOS app. Pure informational — no signup form, no email capture. The goal is to communicate what the app does and who it's for, in a way that matches the app's bold athletic identity.

**Repo:** Separate from the Forge iOS repo. New Next.js project at `~/Projects/forge-web/`.

---

## Visual Style

**Bold Athletic** — dark gradient background, warm orange accent, large uppercase typography. Reference: `full-page-mockup-v2.html`.

- Background: near-black (`#0a0a0a`)
- Accent: orange (`#f97316`)
- Gradient: `linear-gradient(135deg, #111 0%, #1c0a00 60%, #7c2d12 100%)`
- Typography: system font stack (`-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif`), heavy weights (800–900)
- Uppercase section labels with wide letter-spacing
- Radial orange glow effects behind hero
- Dark card surfaces (`#111`) with subtle borders (`#1e1e1e`)

---

## Tech Stack

- **Framework:** Next.js 14+ (App Router)
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS
- **Language:** TypeScript
- **Deployment:** Vercel (free tier)
- **No backend** — fully static export

## Page Structure (Classic One-Pager)

Six sections in order: **Nav → Hero → Problem → How It Works → Features → Who It's For → Footer**

### 1. Nav (sticky)
- Left: Forge flame logo icon + "FORGE" wordmark
- Right: "IN DEVELOPMENT · iOS 17+" pill badge (orange outlined)
- Sticky, semi-transparent with backdrop blur

### 2. Hero
- Pulsing pill badge: "In Development · iOS 17+"
- Headline: **"NO MORE / GUESSWORK."** — "GUESSWORK" in orange
- Subtext: "Forge uses your **body composition data** — not self-reported stats — to generate and continuously adapt a training program built specifically for you."
- 3 iPhone frames (left, center elevated, right) — placeholder areas for real screenshots
  - Labels: Home, AI Plan, Live Session
  - Center frame elevated 20px, orange glow shadow
  - Annotation note: "Real screenshots drop in here"
- Radial orange glow behind the headline

### 3. The Problem
- Section tag: "The Problem"
- Headline: **"Generic apps. / Generic results."**
- Body: "Every fitness app asks how much you weigh and spits out the same routine. Your body composition tells a far more complete story — and no app has been taking it seriously. Until now."
- Two-column comparison grid:
  - **LEFT — Forge** (green dots)
    - Uses real body composition data — muscle mass, body fat %, metabolic rate, and more
    - AI generates a program matched to your actual physiology
    - Re-scans every 4–8 weeks recalibrate the entire program to what your body actually did
    - Adapts intelligently — strength and cardio unified in one plan
  - **RIGHT — Other apps** (red dots)
    - Built on self-reported guesses — weight, height, how you "feel"
    - Same generic routines recycled for every user
    - Plan never changes unless you manually edit it
    - Treat strength and cardio as separate, disconnected products

### 4. How It Works
- Section tag: "How It Works"
- Headline: **"Real data in. / Real programming out."**
- 3-step card grid (equal columns):
  1. **Share your body composition** — Enter body composition results (muscle mass, body fat %, metabolic rate). Forge treats this as the foundation, not an afterthought.
  2. **AI builds your plan** — Artificial intelligence generates a personalised weekly program calibrated to your physiology, training history, and goals. No templates. No guesses.
  3. **It adapts as you do** — Every 4–8 weeks, Forge compares your latest body composition against your last. The AI reads what your body actually changed and rewrites your program to match.
- Each step card: large faded step number, emoji icon, title, body text
- Orange top-border accent on each card

### 5. Features
- Section tag: "Features"
- Headline: **"Everything you need. / Nothing you don't."**
- 3×2 feature card grid:
  1. 🧠 **AI Training Plans** — Generated, evaluated, and regenerated by artificial intelligence based on your body composition and session performance.
  2. 📋 **Body Composition Tracking** — Import your scan results directly. Track body fat %, muscle mass, and metabolic rate over time with trend charts.
  3. ⚡ **Live Session Tracking** — Log sets, reps, and weights in real-time. Auto-detects personal records. Rest timer built in. Works offline.
  4. 🍎 **Apple Health Integration** — Workouts sync to Apple Health automatically. Body weight pre-fills from your latest Apple Health data.
  5. 🏆 **Personal Records** — Auto-detected from your session history. Per-exercise trend charts show your strength progression over time.
  6. 📚 **Exercise Library** — 52 exercises with muscle diagrams, step-by-step instructions, and AI-powered substitutions when you need them.

### 6. Who It's For
- Section tag: "Who It's For"
- Headline: **"Truly personalised. / Not for a type. For you."**
- Two-column layout: editorial statement (left) + audience tag cloud (right)
- Editorial copy:
  > "Most 'personalised' fitness apps personalise the *label* on the same generic plan.
  > Forge is different. Your body composition, your training history, your goals — the AI reads all of it and builds something **that could only be yours**.
  > Whether you've never touched a barbell or you've been training for years, Forge meets you where your body actually is."
- Audience tags (mixed highlighted + neutral):
  - Beginners finding their baseline (highlighted)
  - Experienced lifters breaking plateaus
  - People training strength and cardio (highlighted)
  - Athletes in structured off-seasons
  - Anyone who wants a plan that actually fits
  - Recovery from injury (highlighted)

### 7. Footer
- Forge flame icon + "FORGE" wordmark (centered)
- "Currently in development for iOS 17+"
- "AI-powered · Body composition driven · Apple Health integrated"
- "© 2026 Forge"

| `<Problem>` | Two-column comparison grid |

Each section is its own component in `app/components/`. Page composition happens in `app/page.tsx`.

---

## Screenshot Placeholders

Hero contains 3 iPhone frame placeholders. They render as dark gradient rectangles with italic "Screenshot N" labels and a dashed annotation note. Real screenshots are dropped in later by replacing the placeholder content with `<Image>` components.

---

## Responsive Behavior

- Mobile: single-column for all grids (problem, how-it-works, features, who-it's-for)
- Tablet+: 2-column problem grid, 3-column step/feature grids
- Hero iPhone frames: stack vertically on mobile, horizontal row on desktop
- Nav badge: hidden on very small screens if needed

---

## Language Conventions

- "body composition" — not "InBody" (brand-neutral)
- "AI" or "artificial intelligence" — not "Claude"
- "Apple Health" — not "HealthKit"
- No CTA form, no email signup, no "Download on App Store" link (app not yet released)
- "In Development" messaging throughout

---

## Out of Scope

- Blog, docs, or multi-page site
- Auth, backend, database
- Email capture or waitlist
- Analytics (can be added later)
- App Store link (not released)
